The Challenge

The Wall Street Recruiting Game
Is Rigged Against You

Let's face it: Breaking into investment banking feels impossible when you're staring down the competition. You're from a non-target school. You've got 6 weeks until recruiting starts. And every finance course out there promises to "prepare you for IB"...then buries you in problems you didn't know existed.

Problem #1: Information Overload

Competitors bury you in 70-90 hour courses with every edge case scenario imaginable.

Wall Street Prep teaches you 200 Excel functions. You'll use maybe 15 on the job.

By the time you finish their 5-hour accounting course, you've forgotten where you started. Your brain is fried. And you still can't explain how the three statements connect without looking at your notes.

Problem #2: Static, Boring Learning

Nobody learns well from text-heavy slides with late-90s audio voiceovers.

You can't "see" how the three statements connect by reading bullet points. You can't internalize accretion/dilution by staring at formulas. Your brain needs to SEE concepts in motion, not memorize static definitions.

And god forbid the interviewer asks a curveball question that's not in the 400-question guide everyone memorized.

Problem #3: Interview-Only Training

Most courses get you through the interview...then you hit the desk with zero clue how banking actually works.

Nobody teaches you what a pitch book is. Nobody shows you how to run a bake-off. Nobody explains what to do when the VP hands you a CIM and says "turn this around by Monday."

You end up looking lost on Day 1. Your peers who interned at the same bank are flying, and you're just trying to figure out what "strategic alternatives" even means.

That's exactly why Mike built the Investment Banking Pro Package differently.

When the interviewer asks how depreciation flows through the statements? You'll see the motion in your head. You won't fumble. You won't panic. You'll answer with confidence.

ACCRETION SCALE DIAGRAM

Balance scale with benefits vs costs

Framework #2

The Accretion Scale Framework™
(M&A)

Stop memorizing 47 different scenarios for when deals are accretive or dilutive.

Our M&A course teaches you to see every deal as a visual scale: benefits on one side (earnings added), costs on the other side (price paid). When benefits outweigh costs, the scale tips toward accretion. When costs outweigh benefits, it tips toward dilution.

One mental model. Any deal structure. Cash, stock, debt-financed, doesn't matter. See the scale, solve the deal.

Framework #3

The Asset vs. Cash Flow Decision Tree™
(Valuation)

How do practitioners actually think about valuation?

They start with one question: Is cash flow value greater than asset value?

From there, our visual decision tree walks you through DCF vs. comps vs. precedent transactions. You'll learn WHEN to use each method, not just HOW to calculate them.

This is how Mike valued companies at Golden Gate Capital. This is how real investors think. And this is what separates students who pass interviews from students who nail them.
