## Student psychology drives decision architecture

Finance education purchasing decisions follow systematic patterns revealed through Wall Street Oasis, Reddit, and Quora discussions. The decision framework operates through five sequential filters that prospects apply consciously or unconsciously.

**Second filter: ROI calculation**. Students calculate whether the course will help them get interviews/offers versus cost and time investment. The calculation isn't purely financialâ€”it includes confidence gain, networking access, and risk reduction. **Third filter: urgency multiplier**. Timeline pressure dramatically increases willingness to pay. Critical windows create decision forcing: "If you try to figure it out during the process, you may miss that industry's recruiting window. Roughly 75 percent of Vanderbilt MBA candidates will interview with companies from January through March." **Fifth filter: risk mitigation**. The course should feel comprehensive enough to avoid needing multiple purchases. **Non-target school challenges dominate pain point discussions**.
